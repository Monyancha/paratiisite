Understand How Paratii Works

1P2P file
distribution

Modern servers are good, but swarms can be better. Browser tabs that carry a Paratii player temporarily become light nodes in the network, fetching content from peers alike, as well as from regular non-browser clients - the more sources, better the performance

2Earn while
you browse

If users pay attention for consuming content, they must possibly earn compensation in return. In Paratii, any creator may decide to share his revenue and pay you to watch him. In any case, there’s plenty of other ways to be rewarded without even realizing

3The value of
your opinions

Users who are good curators of either niche or mass content are recognized by a reputation system that rewards “early adopters” and incentivizes exploration of the long tail

4Everyone is
happier

Market indicators show that the retail price of an ad placement in on-demand streaming video is up to a dozen times higher than the current cost of serving all associated data through a decentralized swarm. Without a central entity to cash in the spread as a profit, it all flows back to producers and their audiences

5Embed
anywhere

A javascript application written in meteor.js, the Paratii player can be embedded in any web 2.0 page, and aims to offer native integration with some of the most widespread publishing platforms running on Ethereum. Hint: their official browser is one of them

6Producers control
all of revenue

The medium should not determine how and why videos are to be monetised; the message should: are we talking a feature length pay-per-view movie? An ad-supported short clip? Series designed for subscription? Or free, ad-less amateur content? On Paratii, producers have the power to choose.
